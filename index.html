Correlations Discovered Between Health Risks and Age, Income

As we look at the data, there is a direct correlation between the poverty percentage and the adverse health risks. In general, the higher the percentage of poverty, the higher the likelihood of adverse health risks. There is no discernable correlation between the median age and the various different health risks, but there is an inverse correlation to the median household income and the health risks. As median household income increases, we see a decline in the percentage of individuals in all three categories -- obesity, smoking, and lacking healthcare.
